HUMAN-REVIEW / ESCALATION FLOW
===============================

WHEN DOES A HUMAN GET PULLED IN?
=================================

1. FINANCIAL DECISIONS (Always escalate)
   • Payment deducted but status shows pending (verify transaction ID)
   • Refund requests (process refund, update participant)
   • Invoice/receipt requests (issue official document)
   • Past event certificates missing (verify attendance records)

2. SAFETY & ACCESSIBILITY (Always escalate)
   • Wheelchair/disability accommodation requests (coordinate logistics)
   • Feedback mentioning safety issues, harassment, or financial discrepancies

3. POLICY EXCEPTIONS (Always escalate)
   • Deadline extension requests (only humans decide if emergency is valid)
   • Monetary sponsorship >= ₹25,000 OR with MoU (Treasurer approval required)
   • Alumni sponsorship requesting booth/naming rights (coordinate with CoSA)

4. UNCLEAR/AMBIGUOUS CASES (Sometimes escalate)
   • Mixed registration + sponsorship asks (split into two tracked items)
   • Vague emails with unclear intent (human context-fills)
   • Low AI confidence (< 70%) on any classification (human verifies)

5. SYSTEM FAILURES (Always escalate)
   • Gemini API returns null or errors (safety net—don't silently skip)

---

HOW DO HUMANS KNOW?
===================

1. GMAIL LABEL: "Needs-Review"
   • Escalated email is tagged with this label
   • Humans filter inbox by this label to see all pending decisions

2. STARRED MESSAGE
   • The specific message is starred for visibility

3. STRUCTURED LOGGING
   • Email logged to correct tab: Sponsorship_Log, Support_Log, Accessibility_Log, etc.
   • Log shows: sender, subject, category, sub_category, confidence, reasoning
   • Humans can review context from the log before responding

4. ESCALATION TARGET SPECIFIED
   • Policy sheet maps each issue type to owner:
     - Payment issues → Finance Sub-committee Head
     - Accessibility → Event Logistics + CoSA
     - High-value sponsorship → Treasurer + Faculty Advisor
     - Ambiguous → Relevant Club Lead
   • Email is routed to correct person (via logging + label)

---

HUMAN WORKFLOW (What They Actually Do)
======================================

1. Open Gmail, filter by label: "Needs-Review"
2. Click starred message, read it
3. Open the relevant log sheet (Support_Log, Sponsorship_Log, etc.)
4. Find the email entry — see AI's reasoning + confidence level
5. Make decision (approve, deny, request more info, forward)
6. Reply to sender OR take action (process refund, coordinate logistics, etc.)
7. Update log entry with resolution (optional but good practice)

---

WHAT AI DOES NOT AUTO-REPLY TO
===============================
✗ Payment issues (no "your payment is processing" — humans verify first)
✗ Refund requests (no "refund approved" — humans decide)
✗ Financial/invoice requests (no estimates or promises)
✗ Accessibility requests (no "we'll figure it out" — humans coordinate)
✗ Deadline extensions (no "yes/no" — humans weigh emergencies)
✗ Mixed asks (no partial answers — humans see both)
✗ Low-confidence cases (no guesses — humans verify)
✗ Complaints/sensitive feedback (no defensive responses — humans reply carefully)

---

THE PHILOSOPHY
===============
"When in doubt, escalate. Humans are better at edge cases, judgment calls, 
and anything involving money/safety/ambiguity. AI handles the high-volume, 
low-risk stuff (routine registration questions, spam filtering, 
confirmation resends). Humans handle the rest."

Result: Zero silent failures. Every escalated email has:
• Full context (logged to tab)
• Visual signal (Needs-Review label + star)
• Clear owner (policy specifies who should handle it)
• Complete audit trail (timestamp, reasoning, action taken)
